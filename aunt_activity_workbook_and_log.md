# Activity Workbook & Progress Log

*Companion to the Progressive Activity Plan. This is the one to print and fill in. Effort is judged by the **talk test** (she can always hold a conversation) and **Borg perceived effort (target 11, occasionally 12–13 in later stages)** — not by heart-rate numbers, which don't apply to her right now. Advance only when she's tolerated the current stage comfortably and symptom-free; drop back a stage on any bad day. Confirm progressive exertion with her cardiologist, and let her home EKG readings inform that.*

---

## The stage chart at a glance

| Stage | Focus | Effort (Borg) | Session shape | Aquatic examples | Land strength + balance examples | Ready to advance when… |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **0 — Baseline** *(~1st week)* | Find her true current level | 9–11 | Whatever she already does | Her usual shallow-water walking/movement | Her usual housework, caring for cats | ~1 week of log + EKG data gathered; doctor okays progressing |
| **1 — Build a base** *(~2–3 wks)* | Get comfortable, consistent | 11 (light) | 3–5 min warm-up → ~15–20 min walking in short bouts with rests → 3–5 min cool-down | Chest-deep water walking forward/back; gentle marching in place; slow arm sweeps through water | Sit-to-stands from chair; heel raises (hold counter); ankle pumps; supported single-leg balance — **1 easy set**, 2 days/wk | Session feels light (11), no symptoms, quick recovery, ~1 week |
| **2 — Lengthen time** *(~3–6 wks)* | Endurance | 11–12 | Warm-up → ~10–15 min *continuous* walking, ~20–30 min total → cool-down | Continuous water-walking "laps"; wider strides; gentle leg swings holding the wall | Add **1–2 sets**, light band rows, wall (incline) push-ups, standing hip lifts holding a chair | Longer session feels light, no symptoms, EKG/log stable, ~1 week |
| **3 — Gentle intensity** *(~6–10 wks, only if cleared)* | A little harder, more variety | mostly 11; brief 13 | Warm-up → mostly easy walking with a few **1–2 min faster intervals** → cool-down | Brief faster-walk intervals; side-stepping; gentle high-knee marching; combined arm + leg moves | Light dumbbells/band: seated press, biceps curls; supported low step-ups; longer balance holds | Intervals feel manageable, fast recovery, no symptoms, stable readings |
| **4 — Maintenance** | Keep it for life | 11–13 | ~30+ min comfortable activity, most days | Mix of all the above for variety and enjoyment | ~2 days/wk strength + balance | Ongoing — or transition into a covered, supervised rehab program |

> **Carry-over warm-up / cool-down menu (every session):** marching in place, gentle arm and leg range-of-motion, slow easy walking; finish with slow walking and easy stretches.

*Rule of thumb: never increase two things at once. Lengthen **time** first, then **frequency**, and only much later nudge **intensity**.*

## Keep these visible

**Stop and rest:** breathless enough to interrupt speech, chest discomfort, palpitations, lightheadedness, nausea.

**Call the doctor same day:** sudden weight gain (~2–3 lb in a day or ~5 lb in a week), more swelling, more breathless than usual or needing to prop up to breathe at night, dizziness worse or at rest, persistently low resting rate with symptoms, a worrying EKG.

**Call 911:** chest pain or pressure, fainting or near-fainting, severe shortness of breath, palpitations with feeling faint, bluish lips, confusion. **Dizzy in the water → get to the edge and out (slowly) immediately.**

**Pool, every time:** never alone; phone at the edge; shallow enough to stand head-clear; walk, don't tread; warm not hot/cold water; get out slowly (sit on the steps first); avoid midday heat; hydrate.

**Still her doctor's call, not the plan's:** any beta-blocker change, and diabetes precautions around exercise (hydration, low blood sugar, foot care near the pool).
